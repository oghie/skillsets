# Design Engineering Culture, Structure, And Process

Use this when building or changing engineering culture, career ladder, pods/squads, code review, architecture review, onboarding, incident learning review, or team process.

1. Diagnose scale and risk.
   - Verify: people count, company age, infrastructure size, regulatory/safety risk, and failure patterns are known.
2. Identify repeated failures.
   - Verify: onboarding, promotion, attrition, incident, architecture, delivery, or security failures are evidence-based.
3. Choose structure level.
   - Verify: structure is added to improve learning/transparency, not to copy another company or increase control.
4. Design team topology.
   - Verify: cross-functional and central platform/security/infrastructure needs are balanced.
5. Reserve sustaining capacity.
   - Verify: support, on-call, technical debt, security, refactoring, and tooling have visible allocation.
6. Design processes as risk controls.
   - Verify: code review, architecture review, incident review, access/release rules, and documentation are matched to risk and frequency.
7. Define culture signals.
   - Verify: values, expected behaviors, feedback loops, career ladder, and role-model behaviors are observable.

Output: culture/structure map, process set, career ladder notes, capacity model, and risk controls.
