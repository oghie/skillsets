## Leadership Span
Map:
- direct reports and manager span.
- team missions.
- critical systems owned.
- decision rights.
- succession risk.
- manager maturity.
- overloaded roles.

Red flag: a high-performing engineer is made a tech lead without explicit expectations, mentoring, and authority.

## Hiring And Retention
Hiring plan must connect to portfolio and operating model:
- role needed.
- business capability enabled.
- skills required now vs later.
- onboarding burden.
- manager capacity.
- compensation band.
- retention risk if not hired.

Retention is not only compensation. Review autonomy, clarity, manager quality, growth path, workload, incident burden, recognition, fairness, and belief in strategy.

## Manager System
Expected manager behaviors:
- regular 1:1s with purpose.
- direct feedback before appraisal season.
- delegation with context and authority.
- career development.
- conflict handling.
- performance documentation.
- calibration participation.
- escalation without blame dumping.

## Performance Appraisal
Evaluate:
- outcomes delivered.
- behavior and collaboration.
- technical judgment.
- operational ownership.
- learning and growth.
- leadership at level.
- risk and quality discipline.

Use evidence, not memory. Appraisal periods are biased by recency, visibility, politics, and manager preference.

## 360 Calibration
Collect signals from:
- manager.
- peers.
- direct reports.
- product/business partners.
- security/operations/audit partners when relevant.
- self-review.

Calibration questions:
1. Is the rating evidence-based?
2. Are similar contributions rated similarly?
3. Did invisible operational, security, or mentoring work get valued?
4. Did high-visibility heroics mask preventable chaos?
5. Are bias and proximity effects controlled?

## Incentives
Incentives shape behavior. Check whether current incentives reward:
- shipping without operability.
- hiding security risk.
- local optimization over company outcome.
- hero culture over sustainable systems.
- starting new projects over finishing or retiring work.
- headcount growth over productivity.

## Organization Design
Use Conway-aware mapping:
- Teams should own coherent domains or platforms.
- Interfaces between teams should match architecture boundaries.
- Shared services need clear service model, prioritization, and SLO.
- Security and platform teams should provide guardrails and enablement, not only approval queues.

## Red Flags
- Performance review surprises employees.
- Critical systems depend on one person.
- Manager span is too wide for coaching.
- Career ladder rewards only feature delivery.
- Appraisal ignores security, reliability, mentoring, and operational ownership.
- Reorg is used to avoid hard strategy or personnel decisions.
