## Strategy Red Flags
- Roadmap is a list of projects without business outcome.
- Every initiative is top priority.
- Long-term strategy has no 90-day evidence path.
- Technical debt is acknowledged but never funded.
- Scalability claims lack workload and failure-mode assumptions.

## People Red Flags
- Performance review surprises.
- Managers have too many direct reports.
- Critical systems have no succession.
- Hiring plan ignores onboarding capacity.
- High performers are retained by exceptions, not a healthy system.

## Delivery Red Flags
- Agile rituals exist but priorities constantly churn.
- Teams commit without dependency control.
- Incidents repeatedly interrupt roadmap but capacity model ignores them.
- Delivery metrics improve while customer quality worsens.

## Cybersecurity Red Flags
- Security strategy is tool-first.
- No asset inventory or data classification.
- Risk acceptance is informal.
- IAM joiner/mover/leaver is manual and untracked.
- Backups are not restore-tested.
- Board reporting lacks residual risk and decision asks.
- Audit findings repeat.

## Finance Red Flags
- ROI excludes run cost.
- Cloud cost action is only discount negotiation.
- Vendor concentration has no exit plan.
- Budget cuts remove resilience, security, or maintenance without risk acceptance.

## Governance Red Flags
- Committees have no decision rights.
- Policies are not mapped to controls and evidence.
- Exceptions never expire.
- Audit evidence is recreated manually.

## Culture And Politics Red Flags
- Bad news travels slowly.
- People agree publicly and block privately.
- Heroics are rewarded more than prevention.
- Psychological safety is confused with politeness.
- Leaders use reorgs to avoid hard conversations.

## Response Pattern
When a red flag appears:
1. State the observed signal.
2. Identify the likely system cause.
3. Name the business/security/people risk.
4. Define the smallest useful corrective action.
5. Assign owner and review date.
6. Decide what evidence would prove improvement.
