## Governance Model
Technology governance defines how decisions are made, reviewed, and evidenced.

Core forums:
- Architecture and platform governance.
- Cybersecurity and risk committee.
- Change advisory or release risk review where needed.
- Incident and problem review.
- Data/AI governance.
- Vendor and third-party review.
- Portfolio and investment committee.

Governance must have decision authority. A committee without decision rights becomes theater.

## Risk Management
Risk statement format:

```text
Because [condition], [asset/process/objective] may experience [event],
causing [business impact], with [likelihood/confidence].
Current controls are [controls]. Proposed action is [mitigate/transfer/accept/avoid].
Risk owner is [name/role]. Review date is [date].
```

Use risk appetite to decide if a risk is acceptable. Do not accept risks informally in chat.

## Audit Readiness
Audit readiness requires:
- control owner.
- policy or standard.
- evidence source.
- sampling method.
- retention period.
- exception process.
- remediation owner and due date.
- management review evidence.

If a requirement is jurisdiction-specific or framework-specific, say exactly: `This needs verification.`

## Policy System
Policies should be:
- short enough to read.
- tied to controls and evidence.
- owned by accountable leaders.
- reviewed on cadence.
- enforceable with tooling or operating process.
- exception-aware.

Avoid policy documents that nobody can execute.

## Evidence
Examples:
- access review records.
- change approvals.
- vulnerability remediation evidence.
- incident postmortems.
- backup restore test.
- DR/tabletop exercise.
- architecture exception record.
- vendor due diligence.
- training completion.
- logging/monitoring coverage.

## Reliability And Controls
Borrow risk thinking from high-reliability systems:
- barriers prevent a hazard from becoming loss.
- redundancies reduce single-point failure.
- recoveries reduce impact after failure.
- human reliability depends on workload, clarity, training, and system design.
- organizational reliability depends on incentives, culture, escalation, and leadership attention.

## Red Flags
- Audit is treated as document collection after the fact.
- Risk owner and control owner are unclear.
- Exceptions have no expiry.
- Controls exist but are not tested.
- Evidence is manually reconstructed for every audit.
- The board receives compliance status but not residual risk.
