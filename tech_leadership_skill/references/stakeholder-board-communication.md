## Communication Principle
Different audiences need different altitude:
- Board/shareholders: business outcome, risk, capital allocation, accountability, decision asks.
- CEO/CFO/COO/CRO: trade-offs, cost, risk, delivery, operating impact.
- Product/business peers: customer outcome, sequencing, dependencies, constraints.
- Engineering teams: context, priorities, decision logic, quality bar.
- Audit/regulators: evidence, controls, ownership, remediation.
- Customers/partners: trust, commitments, impact, next steps.

## Board Reporting
Board report should include:
- current state.
- key changes since last report.
- strategic progress.
- material risks.
- cyber posture and incidents if material.
- investment asks.
- decision deadlines.
- metrics with trend.
- actions and owners.

Do not bury a material risk in appendix.

## Executive Advisory
Frame advice as:
1. Decision needed.
2. Options.
3. Recommendation.
4. Trade-offs.
5. Financial impact.
6. Risk impact.
7. People and execution impact.
8. Validation plan.

## Pushback
Push back when:
- requested scope exceeds capacity.
- security/compliance risk is unacceptable.
- deadline requires hidden quality loss.
- budget assumption is false.
- dependency owner is missing.
- business outcome is unclear.

Use evidence and options, not complaint. State what can be true if the leader chooses speed, cost, risk, or scope.

## Presentation
Good executive presentations:
- start with conclusion.
- show one decision per section.
- quantify where evidence exists.
- call out assumptions.
- include risks and mitigations.
- use visuals for systems, roadmap, scorecard, risk map, and operating model.

## Meeting Design
Every leadership meeting needs:
- purpose.
- decision or input needed.
- pre-read.
- owner.
- time box.
- attendees by role.
- output.
- follow-up owner and date.

Cancel recurring meetings that do not create decisions, alignment, learning, or accountability.

## Narrative
Use story ethically:
- current reality.
- tension or risk.
- desired future.
- path and trade-offs.
- role of each stakeholder.
- evidence and next action.

## Red Flags
- Slides show progress but no decision needed.
- Technical detail hides business risk.
- Board cyber report is only compliance status.
- Teams hear decisions after rumors spread.
- Pushback happens too late to be useful.
