# Evidence Synthesis

Use this workflow for literature reviews, systematic reviews, scoping reviews, and meta-analyses.

## Narrative Literature Review Workflow

1. Define the review purpose: background, theory, debate, mechanism, gap, methods, or policy.
2. Define concepts and boundaries.
3. Group sources by theme, mechanism, variable, population, method, or debate.
4. Identify what each group establishes.
5. Compare contradictions and methodological differences.
6. Distinguish strong evidence from weak evidence.
7. State unresolved questions.
8. Connect the review to the user's research question or argument.

## Systematic Review Workflow

1. State the review question.
2. Define population/context, intervention/exposure, comparator, outcome, and study design when applicable.
3. Specify databases and search concepts.
4. Specify date, language, publication type, and field limits.
5. Define inclusion and exclusion criteria before synthesis.
6. Describe screening and study-selection counts.
7. Extract study characteristics and findings consistently.
8. Appraise study quality or risk of bias.
9. Synthesize results by question, design, outcome, and evidence strength.
10. State limitations and implications.

## Meta-Analysis Workflow

1. Confirm studies are comparable enough to pool.
2. Define effect sizes and transformations.
3. Extract and verify effect data.
4. Choose fixed, random, or other model with justification.
5. Assess heterogeneity.
6. Assess publication bias or selective reporting when feasible.
7. Run sensitivity or moderator analysis when warranted.
8. Explain pooled effects in plain language.
9. Avoid policy claims that exceed evidence quality.

## Output

Use:

| Evidence Cluster | Study Types | Main Finding | Quality/Risk | Confidence | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Then provide:
- strongest evidence;
- weakest evidence;
- contradictions;
- missing studies or populations;
- implications for the user's project.
