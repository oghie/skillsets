# Section Evaluation Matrix

Use this file for section-by-section critique of a manuscript or published article.

## Title

Check:
- Specific phenomenon, relationship, population, case, context, or method.
- Reasonable length and intelligible terms for the audience.
- No unexplained acronyms or unnecessary jargon.
- No simple yes/no framing when a statement is clearer.
- No result language unless appropriate for the genre.
- No causal wording unless the design supports it.
- Subtitle adds information rather than repeating the main title.

Revision pattern:

```text
Phenomenon/relationship + population/context + design or distinctive feature
```

## Abstract

Check:
- Purpose, question, or objective.
- Data/sample/context.
- Method/design.
- Key findings.
- Theory if central.
- No unnecessary measure names unless the measure itself is the object of study.
- No vague closing such as only saying implications or future research are discussed.
- Findings and claims match the design.

## Introduction And Literature Review

Check:
- Starts broad enough for importance, then narrows quickly.
- Establishes why the problem matters.
- Defines key concepts.
- Explains theory if relevant and uses it to shape the study.
- Cites factual claims.
- Distinguishes research findings from opinions.
- Synthesizes by issue, concept, debate, mechanism, variable, or method.
- Includes current and contradictory evidence where relevant.
- Critiques strengths and weaknesses of prior work.
- Identifies a specific gap, contradiction, unresolved question, or under-tested mechanism.
- Research question or purpose follows logically from the review.

Red flags:
- Source-by-source summary.
- Large citation pile for one point.
- Direct quotations where synthesis is expected.
- "Gap" claim without showing what the literature already does.
- Theory appears only in a sentence and never returns.

## Sample, Population, And Context

Check:
- Population, case, site, or sampling frame is named.
- Recruitment and inclusion/exclusion criteria are clear.
- Sample demographics or case features are relevant and reported.
- Response rate and nonresponse risks are addressed where applicable.
- Sample size and subgroup size fit the analysis.
- Generalization claims match sampling logic.
- Context-specific studies explain transferability rather than pretending broad generalizability.
- Ethics, consent, and privacy protections are stated where applicable.

## Measures, Data, And Instruments

Check:
- Key concepts have conceptual definitions.
- Operational measures align with concepts.
- Actual items, examples, response formats, sources, or data fields are described enough for evaluation.
- Sensitive-topic measurement addresses social desirability, privacy, wording, and underreporting.
- Observational measures address reactivity and observer influence.
- Reliability evidence fits the measure: internal consistency, temporal stability, interrater/intercoder agreement, or protocol consistency.
- Validity evidence fits the claim: content, face, empirical, criterion, construct, descriptive, interpretive, or theoretical.
- Limitations of measures or data provenance are acknowledged.

## Procedure, Design, And Analysis Plan

Check:
- Design is named and aligned with the question.
- Procedures are detailed enough for replication or evaluation.
- Treatment, intervention, exposure, or field procedure is described precisely when relevant.
- Analysis strategy is suitable for the data and inference.
- Missing data, outliers, transformations, coding, and model choices are transparent when consequential.
- Robustness, sensitivity, validity, or credibility checks are included when risk warrants them.

## Results

Quantitative:
- Descriptives precede inferential tests.
- Percentages include underlying counts where feasible.
- Skewed distributions use median, mode, spread, or appropriate summaries.
- Tables present dense statistics; narrative highlights meaning.
- Effect sizes, uncertainty, and practical significance are addressed.
- Results connect back to research questions, hypotheses, or purposes.
- Figures clarify rather than decorate.

Qualitative:
- Findings are organized analytically, not as a quote collection.
- Data excerpts support interpretation.
- Negative cases, contradictions, or deviant examples are handled.
- Context and participant meanings remain visible.
- The analysis explains how themes, categories, processes, or mechanisms were derived.

Mixed methods:
- Each strand is presented clearly.
- Integration is visible in results, not postponed vaguely.
- Agreements, disagreements, and added insight are explained.

## Discussion And Conclusion

Check:
- Opens with the main answer or contribution for long articles.
- Interprets findings without simply repeating results.
- Connects back to prior literature and theory.
- Avoids introducing major new sources unless necessary.
- Distinguishes evidence-based conclusions from speculation.
- States specific limitations and their effect on interpretation.
- Identifies practical, policy, or theoretical implications when justified.
- Offers specific future research directions tied to limitations or unresolved questions.
- Ends with a restrained contribution statement.

## References And Source Use

Check:
- References support the claims they are attached to.
- Important claims are not uncited.
- Source quality fits the claim.
- Current evidence is used where recency matters.
- Contradictory evidence is not ignored.
- Metadata is plausible and consistent.
- No fabricated, unverifiable, or mismatched sources.
