# Evidence Synthesis And Review

Use this file for literature reviews, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and evidence-base judgments.

## Narrative Literature Reviews

A strong review:
- Organizes by themes, theories, variables, mechanisms, populations, settings, methods, or debates.
- Explains what prior work has established.
- Identifies contradictory findings and methodological differences.
- Distinguishes research evidence from expert opinion.
- Uses current evidence where recency matters and foundational evidence where history matters.
- Critiques strengths and weaknesses without becoming dismissive.
- Shows how the present question follows from what is known and unknown.

Weak review signals:
- One paragraph per source.
- No conceptual definitions.
- No critical comparison across studies.
- No unresolved question, contradiction, or gap.
- Overuse of direct quotations.
- Only supportive sources.
- Claim that the topic is important without evidence.

## Systematic Review

Check:
- Clear review question and scope.
- Databases and search platforms are named.
- Search strings or concepts are transparent enough to reproduce.
- Date range, language restrictions, publication type restrictions, and field boundaries are justified.
- Grey literature, unpublished studies, theses, reports, or trial registries are considered when publication bias is plausible.
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria are defined before interpreting findings.
- Screening process is transparent, ideally with counts at each stage.
- Reasons for exclusion are defensible.
- Study quality or risk-of-bias appraisal is included and used in interpretation.
- Results distinguish quantity of evidence from quality of evidence.

## Meta-Analysis

Check:
- Effect-size selection fits study designs and outcomes.
- Data extraction and coding are transparent.
- Independent coders or verification are used when feasible.
- Heterogeneity is assessed and interpreted.
- Moderator or subgroup analyses are justified.
- Publication bias or selective reporting is assessed when possible.
- Sensitivity analyses test influential studies, alternate models, and assumptions.
- Numerical results are explained in plain language.
- Forest plots, funnel plots, or tables are readable.
- Policy/practice implications account for evidence quality and variability, not only pooled estimates.

## Evidence-Base Judgment

When judging a body of evidence, separate:
- Number of studies.
- Methodological strength.
- Consistency of findings.
- Directness to the target population/context.
- Precision and uncertainty.
- Publication or selective-reporting risk.
- Plausible mechanisms.
- Replication across teams, settings, and methods.

Use cautious conclusions:

```text
The evidence base is suggestive but not conclusive because ...
The strongest studies point toward ...
The evidence is mixed, with stronger designs showing ...
The current evidence supports descriptive/pilot/associational claims, not causal claims.
```
