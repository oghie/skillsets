# AI-Assisted And Ethical Design

Use this for AI-assisted design, generated visual assets, design copilots, AI chat/UI helpers, synthetic UX research summaries, or AI in XR.

## AI Use Cases

- Generate moodboard directions or visual variants.
- Draft microcopy alternatives.
- Summarize research notes.
- Produce placeholder data and content.
- Explore layout options.
- Generate icons, images, 3D assets, sound, or video.
- Prototype code, animations, and shader/material ideas.
- Create conversational characters or virtual guides.
- Personalize content, recommendations, or interface emphasis from user behavior.
- Provide AI chat/help surfaces for task guidance and support.
- Suggest layouts, variants, and experiment candidates for human review.

## Guardrails

| Concern | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Sourcing | Track whether assets are human-made, generated, licensed, public-domain, or unknown. |
| Rights | Do not assume generated output is safe for commercial use. |
| Disclosure | Label visible AI-generated or AI-mediated experiences when trust depends on origin. |
| Privacy | Do not send sensitive user research or customer data into tools without approval. |
| Bias | Review generated personas, imagery, and copy for stereotypes and exclusion. |
| Safety | For XR and social systems, assess behavior, harassment, manipulation, and distress risk. |
| Human review | AI output is draft material until reviewed against UX goals and product constraints. |

## Human-AI Balance

Design should preserve:

- Safety.
- Privacy.
- Transparency.
- Responsibility.
- Governance.
- User control.

AI provides speed, scale, and variation. Humans provide intent, empathy, ethics, context, and accountability.

## Review Questions

- Is the AI contribution visible in the final product?
- Does the user need to know that AI is involved?
- Are generated assets licensed, sourced, or replaceable?
- Could the pattern manipulate, addict, shame, or mislead users?
- Are creators credited or compensated where required?
- Can users opt out, correct, mute, report, or override AI behavior?
- Does the system explain limitations and uncertainty?

## XR-Specific AI Risks

- Embodied agents can feel socially persuasive even when they are automated.
- Generated voices and avatars can imply identity or authority.
- Spatial environments can intensify persuasion, fear, distraction, and fatigue.
- AI-guided navigation or training must be recoverable and explainable.

Require stricter review for AI in immersive environments than for flat UI decoration.
