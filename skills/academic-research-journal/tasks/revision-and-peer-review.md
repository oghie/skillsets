# Revision And Peer Review

Use this workflow for reviewer simulation, editorial strategy, major revision planning, and response letters.

## Reviewer Simulation

Use three lenses unless the user requests another format:

| Reviewer | Lens | Main Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Friendly expert | Contribution, field relevance, framing, theory | Is the paper interesting and situated well? |
| Methods skeptic | Design, sampling, measurement, analysis, overclaiming | Can the evidence support the claims? |
| Editor | Fit, novelty, audience, structure, feasibility of revision | Should this be sent forward, revised, or rejected? |

Output:

```markdown
## Reviewer 1: Contribution and Theory
## Reviewer 2: Methods and Evidence
## Reviewer 3: Editorial Decision
## Consolidated Decision
## Revision Strategy
```

## Revision Strategy

Prioritize:
1. Fatal or rejection-level flaws.
2. Claim-method mismatch.
3. Missing methods, ethics, limitations, or source verification.
4. Contribution and framing.
5. Section organization.
6. Style, formatting, and minor clarity.

Use:

| Priority | Reviewer Risk | Revision | Evidence Needed | Expected Effect |
|---:|---|---|---|---|

## Response Letter Rules

- Be respectful and precise.
- Quote or paraphrase the reviewer concern briefly.
- State the exact action taken.
- Identify where the manuscript changed by section, paragraph, table, or appendix.
- If disagreeing, do so with evidence and humility.
- Never claim a revision was made unless it was actually made.
- Do not overpromise new analyses, data, or citations.

Template:

```markdown
Reviewer comment:
[comment]

Response:
Thank you for this helpful comment. We have addressed it by [specific revision]. In the revised manuscript, we [describe change] in [section/paragraph/table]. This revision clarifies [why it matters].
```

For disagreement:

```markdown
Response:
We agree that [shared concern]. We handled this by [revision]. We did not [requested change] because [evidence-based reason], but we added [limitation/clarification/robustness check] to make the scope of the claim explicit.
```
