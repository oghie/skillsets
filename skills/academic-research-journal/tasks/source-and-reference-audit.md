# Source And Reference Audit

Use this workflow for citation checking, source validity, ethics checks, and journal-specific facts.

## Source Audit Workflow

1. Extract all references and cited claims.
2. Group claims by type: factual background, theory, empirical evidence, method, statistic, policy, or journal fact.
3. Verify source existence and metadata when tools are available.
4. Check whether the source supports the attached claim.
5. Flag unsupported, stale, weak, mismatched, unverifiable, or fabricated-looking sources.
6. Suggest replacement or stronger evidence only when verified or clearly marked as needing verification.

## Journal-Specific Workflow

Current journal facts need live verification:
- aims and scope;
- author guidelines;
- word count;
- article types;
- formatting and citation style;
- reporting standards;
- open access or publication fees;
- required declarations;
- indexing, rankings, metrics, or quartile.

Do not claim journal readiness for a named journal until these are checked or explicitly marked as pending.

## Ethics And Integrity Workflow

Check:
- IRB/ethics review or exemption.
- Consent or justified waiver.
- Privacy, de-identification, and data protection.
- Conflict of interest and funding.
- Sensitive or vulnerable participants.
- Data provenance and permissions.
- Statistical transparency.
- Clear limitations.

|---|---|---|---|---|
