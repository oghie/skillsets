# Evaluate Existing Work

Use this workflow for papers, essays, manuscripts, proposals, literature reviews, systematic reviews, and journal articles.

## Workflow

1. Classify the artifact and intended inference.
2. Extract the research skeleton from `references/journal-readiness-scorecard.md`.
3. Identify missing information as `I/I` and inapplicable criteria as `N/A`.
4. Apply `references/research-quality-principles.md`.
5. Apply `references/section-evaluation-matrix.md` to all relevant sections.
6. Apply method-specific checks from `references/methods-evaluation-matrix.md` or `references/evidence-synthesis-and-review.md`.
7. Audit claims against evidence.
8. Score transparently and recommend concrete revisions.
9. End with a publication-readiness verdict and top priorities.

## Claim Audit

For every important claim, ask:
- What evidence is provided?
- Is the evidence direct or indirect?
- Does the method support the strength of the claim?
- What alternative explanations remain?
- What safer wording is needed?

Use:

| Claim | Evidence | Risk | Safer Wording |
|---|---|---|---|

## Fatal Flaw Criteria

Flag a reject-level or invalidating flaw when:
- The research question and method do not match.
- The main claim requires causality but the design cannot support it.
- Measures do not capture the central construct.
- Data/source provenance is unverifiable or inappropriate.
- Analysis does not answer the research question.
- Core results are missing or internally inconsistent.
- Ethics risk is serious and unaddressed.
- References or reported facts appear fabricated or materially mismatched.

## Output Requirements

- Lead with the most important weakness or verdict.
- Include scores only when useful.
- Give one concrete revision recommendation per major problem.
- Distinguish fixable issues from structural limitations.
- State confidence in the evaluation.
