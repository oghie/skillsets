# Design Research Project

Use this workflow when creating a study, research proposal, empirical validation plan, or method section.

## Research Design Sequence

1. Define the practical or theoretical problem.
2. State the specific research question.
3. Decide the intended inference: exploratory, descriptive, associational, causal, mechanism, predictive, interpretive, policy/practice, or synthesis.
4. Choose the design that fits the inference.
5. Define population, case, setting, or evidence base.
6. Plan sampling, recruitment, or case selection.
7. Define key concepts and operational measures.
8. Specify procedures, data sources, and timing.
9. Choose analysis logic before drafting claims.
10. Identify validity, reliability, credibility, ethics, and feasibility risks.
11. Define expected contribution and claim strength.

## Design Fit Rules

- Use qualitative or exploratory design when the question asks about meanings, experiences, processes, or poorly understood phenomena.
- Use descriptive quantitative design when the question asks how common, how much, how different, or how patterns vary.
- Use causal design only when comparison, temporal order, and confound control can be defended.
- Use mixed methods when each strand answers a distinct part of the question or integration produces added insight.
- Use systematic review or meta-analysis when the claim concerns a body of evidence.
- Use program evaluation when the object is implementation, impact, cost, or policy/practice choice.
