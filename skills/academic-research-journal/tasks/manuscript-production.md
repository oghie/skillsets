Use this workflow for drafting or revising journal articles, essays, thesis chapters, and research manuscripts.

## Contribution Test

A manuscript must answer at least one:
- What do we know now that we did not know before?
- What theory is extended, refined, challenged, or synthesized?
- What empirical pattern is documented?
- What causal mechanism is tested?
- What method, dataset, measure, or framework is improved?
- What policy or practice decision becomes better informed?
- What debate is clarified?

If contribution is vague, propose sharper contribution options before drafting.

## Drafting Rules

- Give every paragraph one job.
- Make every section advance the research question or argument.
- Put contribution early.
- Avoid inflated novelty claims.
- Avoid "fills a gap" unless the gap is specific.
- Use causal language only when the design supports causality.
- Separate evidence, interpretation, and speculation.
- Limit direct quotations; prefer synthesis.
- Do not cite unchecked sources.
- Never fabricate references.

## Section Builders

Introduction:
1. Opening problem.
2. Importance.
3. Existing knowledge.
4. Limitation, contradiction, weak test, or under-theorized mechanism.
5. Study purpose.
6. Contribution.
7. Roadmap.

Literature review:
- What this literature explains well.
- What it misses.
- Where findings conflict.
- What methodological weaknesses recur.
- How it informs the present study.

Results:
- Quantitative: start with descriptives; pair percentages with counts; report magnitude and uncertainty; connect to questions.
- Qualitative: organize by themes, mechanisms, narratives, categories, or processes; analyze evidence; handle deviant cases.
- Mixed methods: show what integration reveals beyond either strand alone.

Discussion:
1. Recap the answer.
2. Explain meaning.
3. Compare with prior literature.
4. State theoretical implications.
5. State practical or policy implications.
6. Discuss limitations honestly.
7. Suggest specific future research.
8. End with a restrained contribution statement.
