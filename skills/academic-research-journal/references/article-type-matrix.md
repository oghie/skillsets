# Article Type Matrix

Use this file when classifying a task, article, manuscript, or research proposal. A paper may occupy more than one row.

## Research Purpose

| Type | Main Question | Typical Evidence | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exploratory | What is happening and how do participants experience it? | Interviews, observations, documents, open-ended data, field immersion | Treating discovery as proof |
| Descriptive | What exists, how common is it, or how does it vary? | Rates, means, proportions, patterns, trends, prevalence | Overexplaining descriptive patterns |
| Explanatory | Why does it happen, or does X affect Y? | Theory, hypotheses, temporal ordering, comparison, causal design | Causal overclaiming |
| Applied | What works for a practical problem? | Program outcomes, implementation data, policy or practice evidence | Ignoring implementation and cost |
| Translational | How can established knowledge be put into practice? | Practice guidance, implementation evidence, stakeholder constraints | Treating theory transfer as direct evidence |

## Empirical Article Families

| Type | What To Look For | Evaluation Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Quantitative | Numerical variables, descriptive or inferential statistics, models, estimates | Sampling, measures, assumptions, effect size, uncertainty, robustness |
| Qualitative | Meanings, contexts, processes, experiences, documents, interviews, observations | Design fit, sampling rationale, coding, reflexivity, credibility, thick evidence |
| Mixed methods | Explicit quantitative and qualitative strands | Strand quality, integration, value added, contradictory findings |
| Experiment | Random assignment, treatment/control, manipulated condition | Assignment, control equivalence, blinding, attrition, compliance, contamination, ethics |
| Quasi-experiment | Comparison without random assignment, natural experiment, before/after, interrupted series | Baseline equivalence, history effects, confounding, sensitivity, alternative explanations |
| Survey | Self-report or administered questionnaire | Question wording, response format, sampling frame, response rate, nonresponse bias, mode effects |
| Field research | Natural setting, observation, real-world intervention, immersed context | Ecological validity, observer influence, access, safety, context detail |
| Big-data/computational | Administrative, platform, sensor, scraped, or large-scale digital traces | Data provenance, missingness, construct validity, bias, reproducibility, ethics |
| Case study | One or few cases selected for analytic value | Case-selection rationale, context, transferability, boundaries, evidence triangulation |
| Action/participatory research | Stakeholders as collaborators, change-oriented inquiry | Stakeholder inclusion, power, ethics, implementation, reflexivity, actionable learning |
| Program/policy evaluation | Program, policy, or intervention effectiveness | Needs, logic model, process, impact, comparison, cost-effectiveness, cost-benefit |
| Pilot study | Preliminary test of design, measure, procedure, or feasibility | Feasibility evidence, limitations, cautious claims, path to larger study |

## Evidence Synthesis And Non-Empirical Work

| Type | What To Look For | Evaluation Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Narrative literature review | Broad synthesis of research, often selective | Organization, source quality, balance, gap logic, critical synthesis |
| Systematic review | Structured search and selection of studies | Search strategy, inclusion/exclusion, screening, study quality, reproducibility |
| Meta-analysis | Statistical synthesis of study results | Effect-size logic, heterogeneity, publication bias, extraction, interpretability |
| Theory article | Concepts, propositions, mechanisms, model | Conceptual clarity, explanatory power, coherence, testability |
| Commentary/opinion/critique | Argument about field, policy, method, or publication | Evidence support, fairness, scope, contribution, transparency |
| Book review | Evaluation of a longer scholarly work | Accuracy, field context, contribution, limitations, audience fit |

## Inference Fit

- Broad population claims need defensible sampling and generalization logic.
- Causal claims need design features that rule out credible alternatives.
- Mechanism claims need evidence on the process linking cause and outcome.
- Interpretive claims need contextual and participant-meaning evidence.
- Practice/policy claims need feasibility, implementation, and cost or trade-off attention.
- Cumulative-evidence claims need systematic synthesis or a justified reading of the body of research.
