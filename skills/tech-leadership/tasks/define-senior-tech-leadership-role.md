# Define Senior Technology Leadership Role

Use this when clarifying CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Engineering, CIO, CISO, Chief Architect, technical founder, or senior technology executive responsibilities.

1. Identify the business stage and technology need.
   - Verify: current company stage, sector, risk, product maturity, and growth constraints are explicit.
2. Map required leadership capabilities.
   - Verify: R&D, technology strategy, organization, execution, external face, infrastructure/operations, and business executive work are each assigned or marked as gaps.
3. Separate CTO and VP Engineering expectations.
   - Verify: strategy, execution, people management, architecture, external representation, and resource authority are not conflated.
4. Define technical True North.
   - Verify: production readiness, testing, scaling, architecture, security, operational oversight, and acceptable exceptions are stated.
5. Clarify authority.
   - Verify: reporting line, budget, people allocation, risk acceptance, roadmap influence, and decision rights are known.
6. Assess executive peer trust.
   - Verify: conflict handling, disagreement/commit behavior, board/CEO communication, and cross-functional partnership are reviewed.
7. Define evidence and cadence.
   - Verify: dashboard, decision forums, role review date, and success measures are written.

Output: senior role map, True North, decision rights, risks, and first 90-day focus.
