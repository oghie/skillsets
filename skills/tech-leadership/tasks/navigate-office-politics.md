# Navigate Office Politics Ethically

Use this when informal power, stakeholder conflict, hidden blockers, reputation risk, cross-functional friction, or executive misalignment affects technology work.

1. State the material issue.
   - Verify: business/technology/security outcome at risk is explicit.
2. Map stakeholders.
   - Verify: formal power, informal influence, incentives, fears, dependencies, and likely objections are listed.
3. Separate evidence from interpretation.
   - Verify: motives are not invented; assumptions are named.
4. Identify ethical options.
   - Verify: options avoid deception, retaliation, manipulation, and confidentiality breaches.
5. Build alignment path.
   - Verify: sequence of conversations, sponsors, neutral facts, trade-offs, and decision forum are defined.
6. Prepare pushback.
   - Verify: non-negotiables, fallback options, and escalation route are clear.
7. Protect trust.
   - Verify: communication is consistent, documented where needed, and respectful of local/cultural context.

Output: stakeholder map, risk map, conversation plan, decision path, and escalation plan.
