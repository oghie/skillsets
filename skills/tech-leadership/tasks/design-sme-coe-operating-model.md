# Task: Design SME And COE Operating Model

## Goal
Create a practical expert-capability model that improves decision quality and capability reuse without creating bottlenecks.

## Required Inputs
- Organization stage, sector, geography, regulation, and team topology.
- Capability or domain needing expertise.
- Current pain: repeated decisions, audit findings, incidents, duplicated work, weak standards, or scarce expertise.
- Current experts, platform teams, governance forums, and communities of practice.
- Decision rights, funding path, and operational owner.

## Steps
1. Classify the need as SME, COE, both, or neither.
2. Define mission, non-mission, users, service catalog, and decision rights.
3. Select COE mode if needed: advisory, enablement, platform/service, governance, or community of practice.
4. Define intake, response time, review depth, exception process, and escalation path.
5. Create first reusable assets: checklist, reference pattern, template, training, runbook, or golden path.
6. Define metrics for adoption, review cycle time, repeated questions, exceptions, incidents/findings reduced, and team usefulness.
7. Define federation or sunset strategy.

## Verification
- Every approval gate has explicit authority and scope.
- Teams still own delivery unless ownership is intentionally transferred.
- SME time is not a single point of failure.
- COE success is measured by adoption and outcomes, not documents.
- Exceptions have owner, expiry, and review cadence.

## Output
Use `templates/sme-coe-operating-model.md` and optionally `templates/mermaid-sme-coe-operating-model.mmd`.
