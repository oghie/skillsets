## Operating Model
A technology leader needs a repeatable operating system:

| Layer | Purpose | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Business objectives | Define why technology work matters. | Strategy, revenue/cost/risk goals, customer commitments |
| Decision rights | Prevent hidden ownership gaps. | RACI, delegated authorities, escalation map |
| Portfolio | Allocate capacity across run, grow, transform, and risk reduction. | Roadmap, capacity model, dependency map |
| Operating cadence | Convert intent into reviewed action. | Weekly review, monthly scorecard, quarterly planning |
| Controls | Keep delivery, security, cost, and resilience inside risk appetite. | Policies, guardrails, audit evidence, risk register |
| Feedback | Learn from delivery, incidents, customers, people, and financials. | Postmortems, surveys, metrics, retrospectives |

## Cadence
- Daily: blockers, incidents, critical decisions, executive escalations, customer or regulator exposure.
- Weekly: delivery predictability, security/risk exceptions, operational health, hiring/retention, cross-functional dependencies.
- Monthly: KPI dashboard, budget burn, vendor spend, technical debt movement, audit evidence, roadmap change.
- Quarterly: strategy review, portfolio reallocation, org design, succession, cyber posture, resilience drill, board pack.
- Annual: multi-year roadmap, funding model, target operating model, regulatory plan, platform/infrastructure strategy.

## Control Loops
- Strategy loop: objective -> options -> decision -> execution -> evidence -> adjustment.
- Delivery loop: plan -> capacity -> commitment -> execution -> blocked work -> recovery.
- Risk loop: identify -> assess -> control -> test -> accept or remediate -> report.
- People loop: expectation -> feedback -> development -> appraisal -> compensation -> succession.
- Finance loop: business case -> budget -> spend -> value realization -> reforecast.

## Leadership Dashboard
Track leading and lagging indicators. Avoid dashboards that show only activity.

Core panels:
- Business value: revenue enablement, customer impact, cost avoidance, strategic capability.
- Delivery: predictability, cycle time, lead time, blocked work, dependency age, escaped defects.
- Reliability: availability, SLO, incident count/severity, MTTR, repeat incidents, on-call load.
- Cybersecurity: critical asset coverage, MFA/PAM, vulnerability age, detection coverage, incident readiness, audit findings.
- Finance: run/grow/transform spend, cloud/vendor variance, unit cost, budget forecast, ROI evidence.
- People: regretted attrition, hiring pipeline, manager span, engagement signal, calibration fairness, succession risk.

## Daily Leadership Triage
Ask:
1. What can harm customers, employees, money, compliance, security, or reputation today?
2. Which decision is blocked by unclear ownership?
3. Which executive expectation is misaligned with capacity or risk?
4. Which metric changed and needs explanation?
5. Which issue needs communication before rumor fills the gap?

## Red Flags
- Leadership work is only meetings, not decisions and evidence.
- Every decision escalates upward because rights are unclear.
- Dashboards report activity but not outcomes or risks.
- Cybersecurity is absent from operating cadence until audit or incident.
- Roadmap commitments ignore operational load and technical debt.
- People processes exist but managers do not use them consistently.
