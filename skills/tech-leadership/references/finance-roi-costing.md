## Financial Frame
Translate technology work into:
- revenue enablement.
- margin improvement.
- cost avoidance.
- risk reduction.
- resilience.
- compliance.
- customer retention.
- speed to market.
- strategic optionality.

Avoid fake precision. State assumptions and confidence.

## Budget Categories
- Run: operations, licenses, support, cloud baseline, security operations, maintenance.
- Grow: product delivery, customer features, integration, analytics.
- Transform: platform modernization, architecture, AI/data capability, automation.
- Protect: cybersecurity, reliability, audit remediation, technical debt, disaster recovery.

## ROI And Value Realization
ROI needs:
- baseline.
- expected benefit.
- implementation cost.
- operating cost.
- adoption dependency.
- risk adjusted confidence.
- time to value.
- owner of benefit realization.

For AI and data work, include data readiness, model evaluation, governance, adoption, and ongoing monitoring costs.

For cybersecurity, ROI is often risk reduction, audit readiness, customer trust, resilience, or contractual enablement. Do not force it into direct revenue when that is not the real logic.

## Cost Control
Review:
- cloud spend variance.
- unused licenses.
- vendor overlap.
- support contracts.
- data/storage growth.
- incident cost.
- rework cost.
- technical debt drag.
- manual compliance evidence cost.

## Vendor And Partner Spend
For build/buy/partner:
- strategic importance.
- differentiation.
- integration cost.
- switching cost.
- data/security risk.
- vendor viability.
- SLA and support.
- contractual audit/security terms.
- exit plan.

Current vendor pricing, roadmap, security claims, and contract terms require live verification. Say exactly: `This needs verification.`

## Investment Decision
Use this decision test:
1. What business outcome changes?
2. What risk is reduced or accepted?
3. What cost is one-time vs recurring?
4. What capability remains after the project?
5. What must be stopped to fund this?
6. What is the review date for value evidence?

## Red Flags
- Budget cuts target only visible spend while increasing operational risk.
- Cloud cost is optimized without architecture and product usage analysis.
- Cyber spend is tool-led and not tied to risk appetite.
- ROI excludes run cost and adoption work.
- Vendor consolidation ignores lock-in and resilience.
